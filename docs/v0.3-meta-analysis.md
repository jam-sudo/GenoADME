# v0.3 Meta-Analysis: SLCO1B1/Pravastatin PM/EM AUC Ratio

> Research and educational tool only; not FDA-cleared.

This document is the **pre-registered protocol** for the v0.3 meta-analysis of clinical SLCO1B1/pravastatin PM/EM AUC ratio data. The protocol section is committed BEFORE any literature search is executed; the findings section is appended only after data extraction is complete.

The pre-registration discipline mirrors [`docs/holdout-seed.md`](holdout-seed.md): inclusion/exclusion criteria, extraction schema, and analysis method are locked before the data shape is known, so they cannot be selected post-hoc to favour a particular outcome.

The motivating question is in [`docs/limitations.md` §11](limitations.md): the v0.2 GenoADME prediction PM/EM AUC ratio is 4.482, outside the pre-specified band [1.4, 2.5]. Two resolution paths are open:

- **(11.3a)** Per-substrate CPIC scale calibration with empirical justification → Sisyphus-side change.
- **(11.3b)** Re-spec the [1.4, 2.5] PM/EM AUC band → GenoADME-side `tier-change:` commit, allowed only with empirical justification.

Either path needs a defensible literature estimate of the **clinical** PM/EM AUC ratio. This meta-analysis produces that estimate.

## 1. Pre-registered protocol

**Date locked:** 2026-05-04.
**Status:** Locked. Any post-lock change to this section is recorded in §1.7 with reason and timestamp.

### 1.1 Research question

In healthy adult humans receiving a single oral dose of pravastatin, what is the mean pharmacokinetic AUC ratio between SLCO1B1 Poor-Function homozygous (`*5/*5` or `c.521 C/C`) carriers and SLCO1B1 Normal-Function homozygous (`*1/*1` or `c.521 T/T`) non-carriers?

This is the **PM/EM AUC ratio** in the GenoADME Tier 1 spec. Heterozygous (IM, `*1/*5` or `c.521 T/C`) carriers are extracted as a secondary metric but do not enter the primary pooled estimate.

### 1.2 Search strategy

**Sources:**
- WebSearch (general engine, includes PubMed/Scholar surfacing).
- WebFetch on each candidate paper's abstract URL (PubMed, publisher).
- bioRxiv preprint server via the `claude_ai_bioRxiv` MCP (for unpublished manuscripts).
- Anchor papers cited as known: Niemi 2006 (`PMID 17047488`), Pasanen 2007.

**Search queries (verbatim, captured during execution):**
The set is finalized in §2.1 of the findings section after execution. Pre-spec tokens:

- `SLCO1B1 pravastatin pharmacokinetics 521`
- `SLCO1B1 *5 pravastatin AUC homozygous`
- `OATP1B1 pravastatin polymorphism healthy volunteers`
- `rs4149056 pravastatin exposure`

**Date range:** 2000–2026 (SLCO1B1 was first cloned in 1999; rs4149056 PK studies postdate that).

**Languages:** English-only (extraction requires reading methods/results sections).

### 1.3 Inclusion criteria

A study is **included** if it satisfies all of the following:

1. **Original research** (not a review, editorial, or prior meta-analysis). Reviews are used only to discover candidate primary studies via their reference lists.
2. **Healthy adult human volunteers** (≥ 18 years, no concurrent statin therapy, no clinical condition affecting hepatic clearance). Studies in patients with hepatic impairment or polypharmacy are excluded.
3. **Single oral dose of pravastatin** at any dose ≥ 10 mg. Multiple-dose / steady-state studies are excluded — the GenoADME Tier 1 spec is anchored on single-dose exposure.
4. **SLCO1B1 genotype reported** for each individual or each phenotype group, allowing assignment to PM (`*5/*5` or `c.521 C/C`), IM (`*1/*5` or `c.521 T/C`), and EM (`*1/*1` or `c.521 T/T`).
5. **AUC reported** as group mean ± SD (or SE, IQR with N) for at least the EM and PM groups. Studies that report only AUC ratios without component values are kept if (a) the ratio is for PM/EM specifically and (b) a CI or SE on the ratio is reported.
6. **N ≥ 4 per relevant phenotype group** (EM and PM). Studies with N=1–3 PM are excluded from the primary estimate but retained as supplementary observations.

A study is **excluded** if any of:

- Pediatric or pregnant cohorts.
- Statin-statin or statin-CYP-inhibitor co-administration in the PK arm.
- Pravastatin combined with another active drug as a fixed dose.
- AUC measured only after IV administration (the OATP1B1 effect is on hepatic uptake; oral PK is the right context).
- SLCO1B1 phenotype assignment based on a non-rs4149056 marker without confirmation that the marker tags the *5 allele in the cohort's ancestry.

### 1.5 Analysis method

**Primary estimate: pooled log-ratio random-effects.**

For each study with both EM and PM group means and SDs reported, compute the log PM/EM AUC ratio and its variance using the delta-method approximation:

```
log_ratio = ln(pm_auc_mean / em_auc_mean)
var(log_ratio) ≈ (sd_pm / pm_auc_mean / sqrt(n_pm))^2
              + (sd_em / em_auc_mean / sqrt(n_em))^2
```

Pool with the DerSimonian-Laird random-effects estimator (between-study variance τ² estimated from method-of-moments). Back-transform the pooled log-ratio to give pooled ratio + 95% CI.

**Heterogeneity:** report I² (Higgins) and Cochran's Q with df.

**Sensitivity analyses (pre-specified):**

1. Restrict to studies with `extraction_confidence == "high"`. The primary pooled estimate uses all included studies; this sensitivity drops anything not extracted from a paper's tables.
2. Leave-one-out: re-pool excluding each study in turn, report the range of pooled ratios. Identifies any single-study dominance.
3. Restrict to `dose_mg == 40` (the most common dose; matches the GenoADME Tier 1 simulation dose). Tests whether the dose-aggregated pool is dose-confounded.

**Decision rules (pre-specified, applied to the primary pooled estimate):**

- **D-A (path 11.3a confirmed):** if pooled CI is entirely inside [1.4, 2.5] → the v0.2 spec band is empirically supported, and the model output 4.482 is a model defect requiring per-substrate CPIC scale calibration on the Sisyphus side.
- **D-B (path 11.3b possible):** if pooled CI extends materially above 2.5 (e.g. upper bound > 3.5) → there is empirical room for a wider band re-spec. The exact widened band would itself require evidence-driven specification.
- **D-C (mixed):** pooled CI overlaps but is not entirely inside [1.4, 2.5] → both paths remain open; user judgment needed.

These rules are applied **once**, to the primary estimate, before any sensitivity analysis. Sensitivity analyses inform robustness but do not over-write the primary decision.

### 1.6 What this protocol is NOT

This is a **literature review with structured extraction and a simple pooled estimate**, not a formal Cochrane-style meta-analysis. Specifically:

- **No PROSPERO registration.** The pre-registration is internal to this repository.
- **No grey-literature or non-English search.** Conference abstracts and non-English studies are missed by design — disclosed as a limitation.
- **Full-text access is best-effort.** Where only abstracts are accessible, extraction is from the abstract; this is recorded as `medium` confidence.
- **No risk-of-bias scoring.** Studies are characterized by `extraction_confidence` (which captures source-quality coarsely), not a formal QUADAS / Newcastle-Ottawa instrument.
- **No effect modification analysis** beyond the pre-specified dose-stratified sensitivity.

These limitations are accepted because (a) the goal is to inform a decision between two well-defined paths (11.3a vs 11.3b), not to publish a standalone meta-analysis, and (b) a more formal protocol would inflate scope beyond v0.3 milestone reasonable bounds. The honest reporting of these limitations is what makes the resulting decision defensible.

### 2.3 Primary estimate

**Single-study only.** Of all primary studies satisfying §1.3 inclusion criteria with N≥4 PM, **only Niemi 2006 has an extractable PM/EM AUC ratio with confidence interval**, and only as a sex-stratified men-only sub-cohort:

- **Niemi 2006, men-only stratum** (N(c.521 T/T)=8; N(c.521 C/C)=3): PM/EM AUC₀₋∞ ratio = **3.32**, 95% CI **[1.74, 4.91]**, derived from the abstract's "232% greater (95% CI 74–391%, P = .002)" finding via 1 + percent/100.
- The sex-pooled Niemi 2006 estimate is **not** in the accessible abstract and could not be verified.
- Group-mean AUC values and SDs were not in the accessible abstract — this prevented delta-method variance computation from raw means and therefore prevented a formal random-effects pool of multiple studies.

**Honest framing:** the §1.5 protocol pre-specified a DerSimonian-Laird random-effects pool. The actual extractable evidence base contains exactly one primary CI from one sub-stratum of one study. A formal pool is not warranted on this evidence; the Niemi 2006 men-stratum 95% CI [1.74, 4.91] is the best available empirical anchor and is reported as such, with the single-study, sex-stratified, N(CC)=3 caveat surfaced explicitly.

**Indirect/supplementary observations** (not entered into the primary estimate; recorded in the TSV's `notes` column):

- Ho 2007 N(PM)=2 AUC₀₋₅ ratio: **2.02** (no CI; SD on PM unreliable at N=2; window mismatched with primary).
- Cited review-level claim (search results, not a primary extraction): "AUCs of pravastatin have been **57–130% higher** in rs4149056 CC homozygotes than in TT homozygotes" — corresponds to **ratio range 1.57–2.30**.
- Niemi 2006 men-only PM/EM **Cmax** ratio (separate metric): 3.74× (from abstract's "274% greater" finding).
- Mwinyi 2004 IM (*1a/*5, c.521 T/C) AUC vs *1a/*1a EM: ratio 1.42 (heterozygous, not PM/EM).

The supplementary observations broadly support the direction of the SLCO1B1 PM effect but do not give a tighter PM/EM AUC ratio estimate than Niemi 2006's wide CI.

### 2.4 Sensitivity analyses

The pre-specified sensitivity analyses (high-confidence subset; leave-one-out; dose ≤ 40 mg restriction) are not separately informative here — there is only one primary estimate, all from a 40 mg single-dose protocol, and `extraction_confidence` is `medium` because the values come from the abstract not from a paper's table. Sensitivity analysis #1 (high-confidence subset) would drop Niemi 2006 and leave the primary estimate empty.

This is itself a finding: **the empirical evidence base for the SLCO1B1/pravastatin PM/EM AUC ratio at N≥4 PM is essentially a single study's sex-stratified sub-cohort with a wide CI**.

### 2.5 Decision rule applied

The pre-spec decision rule (§1.5) compares the **primary pooled CI** to the v0.2 spec band [1.4, 2.5]. With only one primary CI to apply the rule against:

| rule  | pre-spec definition                                    | result against Niemi 2006 men-stratum CI [1.74, 4.91]     |
|-------|--------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| D-A   | CI entirely inside [1.4, 2.5]                          | **FALSE.** Niemi CI extends to 4.91, well outside band.    |
| D-B   | CI upper bound > 3.5                                   | **TRUE.** Niemi CI upper = 4.91.                           |
| D-C   | CI overlaps band but is not entirely inside            | **TRUE.** [1.74, 2.5] overlap is partial.                  |

**Both D-B and D-C fire** — they describe the same situation from different angles ("there is empirical room for a wider band" + "both paths remain partially open"). Neither D-A (clean path 11.3a — model defect) nor pure D-B (clean path 11.3b — band re-spec) applies in isolation.

**Critical empirical observation** (not pre-specified, but a direct consequence of the rule application): the v0.2 model output PM/EM AUC ratio = **4.482** is **inside Niemi 2006 men-stratum's 95% CI [1.74, 4.91]**. The model's central tendency is at the upper edge of the only accessible primary empirical CI for this metric. This means the v0.2 PARTIAL verdict's failing criterion is *not* statistically inconsistent with the only primary single-dose CC-vs-TT N≥4 evidence we could extract.

Equally critically: the original v0.1.0 spec band [1.4, 2.5] was justified in [`docs/validation-tiers.md`](validation-tiers.md) by citing "Niemi 2006 and Pasanen 2007 clinical observations of 1.6–2.0×". The Pasanen 2007 paper is on **atorvastatin and rosuvastatin, not pravastatin** (PMID 17473846 — confirmed via search). The "1.6–2.0×" range appears to come from review-level summaries that aggregate over haplotype-equivalents or non-CC genotypes, not from primary single-dose CC-vs-TT studies with N≥4. The pre-spec band [1.4, 2.5] is therefore tighter than the primary literature with N≥4 PM directly supports.

### 2.6 Recommendation — proposed path forward

Three options, in order of caution:

- **(P-i) Re-spec band to [1.4, 5.0] via `tier-change:`.** Justified by Niemi 2006 men-stratum 95% CI [1.74, 4.91]; full transparency that the empirical anchor is single-study, sex-stratified, N(CC)=3. v0.2 model output 4.482 then PASSES the widened band. v0.3 closes via `metric-change:`. **Risk:** widening on a single sub-cohort is one Sisyphus calibration update away from looking like face-saving even if today the call is honest.

- **(P-ii) Pursue path (a) Sisyphus per-substrate calibration without changing the band.** Bring the model central tendency from 4.482 toward the 2.0–3.5 range that the broader (sparser) literature indicates is typical. v0.3 stays open until Sisyphus delivers. **Tradeoff:** depends on Sisyphus-side work; v0.3 closure is conditional on user/Sisyphus prioritization. The closing condition's "all three criteria PASS" requirement is met without a band change.

- **(P-iii) Document findings and keep v0.3 open with revised closing condition.** Update the v0.3 milestone to require new primary empirical data — e.g., a fresh clinical PK study with N(PM)≥10, or a pooled re-analysis of open individual-patient PK datasets — before either band re-spec or model calibration is committed. The current evidence is too sparse for either.

**Recommendation: (P-ii).** Reasoning: (P-i) is technically defensible but uncomfortably close to face-saving on single-study evidence; (P-iii) is overly conservative because Sisyphus-side calibration is a real engineering improvement that can be pursued regardless of the meta-analysis outcome. (P-ii) is the path that both improves the model and preserves the conservative v0.1.0 spec band as the target. If Sisyphus calibration succeeds in bringing the central tendency to ~2.5–3.5, the question of band-widening becomes less urgent; if it cannot reach the band, (P-i) can still be pursued later with stronger framing ("we tried calibration first").

User decision required:
1. Endorse (P-ii)? If yes, the next concrete step is filing a Sisyphus-side issue requesting per-substrate CPIC scale calibration for OATP1B1 substrates with empirical anchor at clinical PM/EM AUC ratio, citing this analysis.
2. Or override to (P-i) or (P-iii)? Each is defensible with the documented caveats.

### 2.7 Decision recorded — P-ii enacted

**Date:** 2026-05-04. **User decision:** P-ii.

The capability request is filed as [Sisyphus #31 — "Per-substrate effective phenotype scale override hook (downstream calibration injection)"](https://github.com/jam-sudo/Sisyphus/issues/31). Framed as a capability addition (not a calibration defect report) so the closure of Sisyphus #8 ("community-standard, not a Sisyphus-specific defect") stands cleanly. The new issue requests an opt-in `phenotype_scale_overrides=` keyword on `apply_phenotype_to_graph()` — when omitted, today's behavior is unchanged; when supplied, GenoADME injects an empirically-calibrated effective scale for the (gene, phenotype, substrate) tuple.

GenoADME [v0.3 milestone](https://github.com/jam-sudo/GenoADME/milestone/3) description updated to record Phase 1 complete, P-ii enacted, and Sisyphus #31 as the gating dependency.

**Fallback policy** (in case Sisyphus #31 is closed won't-fix or stalled): revert to P-i (band re-spec via `tier-change:`, single-study caveats fully disclosed) or P-iii (await new primary empirical data). The choice between fallbacks is itself a user decision and would require re-engaging this document's §2.6.
