# v0.3 Meta-Analysis: SLCO1B1/Pravastatin PM/EM AUC Ratio

> Research and educational tool only; not FDA-cleared.

This document is the **pre-registered protocol** for the v0.3 meta-analysis of clinical SLCO1B1/pravastatin PM/EM AUC ratio data. The protocol section is committed BEFORE any literature search is executed; the findings section is appended only after data extraction is complete.

The pre-registration discipline mirrors [`docs/holdout-seed.md`](holdout-seed.md): inclusion/exclusion criteria, extraction schema, and analysis method are locked before the data shape is known, so they cannot be selected post-hoc to favour a particular outcome.

The motivating question is in [`docs/limitations.md` §11](limitations.md): the v0.2 GenoADME prediction PM/EM AUC ratio is 4.482, outside the pre-specified band [1.4, 2.5]. Two resolution paths are open:

- **(11.3a)** Per-substrate CPIC scale calibration with empirical justification → Sisyphus-side change.
- **(11.3b)** Re-spec the [1.4, 2.5] PM/EM AUC band → GenoADME-side `tier-change:` commit, allowed only with empirical justification.

Either path needs a defensible literature estimate of the **clinical** PM/EM AUC ratio. This meta-analysis produces that estimate.

## 1. Pre-registered protocol

**Date locked:** 2026-05-04.
**Status:** Locked. Any post-lock change to this section is recorded in §1.7 with reason and timestamp.

### 1.1 Research question

In healthy adult humans receiving a single oral dose of pravastatin, what is the mean pharmacokinetic AUC ratio between SLCO1B1 Poor-Function homozygous (`*5/*5` or `c.521 C/C`) carriers and SLCO1B1 Normal-Function homozygous (`*1/*1` or `c.521 T/T`) non-carriers?

This is the **PM/EM AUC ratio** in the GenoADME Tier 1 spec. Heterozygous (IM, `*1/*5` or `c.521 T/C`) carriers are extracted as a secondary metric but do not enter the primary pooled estimate.

### 1.2 Search strategy

**Sources:**
- WebSearch (general engine, includes PubMed/Scholar surfacing).
- WebFetch on each candidate paper's abstract URL (PubMed, publisher).
- bioRxiv preprint server via the `claude_ai_bioRxiv` MCP (for unpublished manuscripts).
- Anchor papers cited as known: Niemi 2006 (`PMID 17047488`), Pasanen 2007.

**Search queries (verbatim, captured during execution):**
The set is finalized in §2.1 of the findings section after execution. Pre-spec tokens:

- `SLCO1B1 pravastatin pharmacokinetics 521`
- `SLCO1B1 *5 pravastatin AUC homozygous`
- `OATP1B1 pravastatin polymorphism healthy volunteers`
- `rs4149056 pravastatin exposure`

**Date range:** 2000–2026 (SLCO1B1 was first cloned in 1999; rs4149056 PK studies postdate that).

**Languages:** English-only (extraction requires reading methods/results sections).

### 1.3 Inclusion criteria

A study is **included** if it satisfies all of the following:

1. **Original research** (not a review, editorial, or prior meta-analysis). Reviews are used only to discover candidate primary studies via their reference lists.
2. **Healthy adult human volunteers** (≥ 18 years, no concurrent statin therapy, no clinical condition affecting hepatic clearance). Studies in patients with hepatic impairment or polypharmacy are excluded.
3. **Single oral dose of pravastatin** at any dose ≥ 10 mg. Multiple-dose / steady-state studies are excluded — the GenoADME Tier 1 spec is anchored on single-dose exposure.
4. **SLCO1B1 genotype reported** for each individual or each phenotype group, allowing assignment to PM (`*5/*5` or `c.521 C/C`), IM (`*1/*5` or `c.521 T/C`), and EM (`*1/*1` or `c.521 T/T`).
5. **AUC reported** as group mean ± SD (or SE, IQR with N) for at least the EM and PM groups. Studies that report only AUC ratios without component values are kept if (a) the ratio is for PM/EM specifically and (b) a CI or SE on the ratio is reported.
6. **N ≥ 4 per relevant phenotype group** (EM and PM). Studies with N=1–3 PM are excluded from the primary estimate but retained as supplementary observations.

A study is **excluded** if any of:

- Pediatric or pregnant cohorts.
- Statin-statin or statin-CYP-inhibitor co-administration in the PK arm.
- Pravastatin combined with another active drug as a fixed dose.
- AUC measured only after IV administration (the OATP1B1 effect is on hepatic uptake; oral PK is the right context).
- SLCO1B1 phenotype assignment based on a non-rs4149056 marker without confirmation that the marker tags the *5 allele in the cohort's ancestry.

### 1.5 Analysis method

**Primary estimate: pooled log-ratio random-effects.**

For each study with both EM and PM group means and SDs reported, compute the log PM/EM AUC ratio and its variance using the delta-method approximation:

```
log_ratio = ln(pm_auc_mean / em_auc_mean)
var(log_ratio) ≈ (sd_pm / pm_auc_mean / sqrt(n_pm))^2
              + (sd_em / em_auc_mean / sqrt(n_em))^2
```

Pool with the DerSimonian-Laird random-effects estimator (between-study variance τ² estimated from method-of-moments). Back-transform the pooled log-ratio to give pooled ratio + 95% CI.

**Heterogeneity:** report I² (Higgins) and Cochran's Q with df.

**Sensitivity analyses (pre-specified):**

1. Restrict to studies with `extraction_confidence == "high"`. The primary pooled estimate uses all included studies; this sensitivity drops anything not extracted from a paper's tables.
2. Leave-one-out: re-pool excluding each study in turn, report the range of pooled ratios. Identifies any single-study dominance.
3. Restrict to `dose_mg == 40` (the most common dose; matches the GenoADME Tier 1 simulation dose). Tests whether the dose-aggregated pool is dose-confounded.

**Decision rules (pre-specified, applied to the primary pooled estimate):**

- **D-A (path 11.3a confirmed):** if pooled CI is entirely inside [1.4, 2.5] → the v0.2 spec band is empirically supported, and the model output 4.482 is a model defect requiring per-substrate CPIC scale calibration on the Sisyphus side.
- **D-B (path 11.3b possible):** if pooled CI extends materially above 2.5 (e.g. upper bound > 3.5) → there is empirical room for a wider band re-spec. The exact widened band would itself require evidence-driven specification.
- **D-C (mixed):** pooled CI overlaps but is not entirely inside [1.4, 2.5] → both paths remain open; user judgment needed.

These rules are applied **once**, to the primary estimate, before any sensitivity analysis. Sensitivity analyses inform robustness but do not over-write the primary decision.

### 1.6 What this protocol is NOT

This is a **literature review with structured extraction and a simple pooled estimate**, not a formal Cochrane-style meta-analysis. Specifically:

- **No PROSPERO registration.** The pre-registration is internal to this repository.
- **No grey-literature or non-English search.** Conference abstracts and non-English studies are missed by design — disclosed as a limitation.
- **Full-text access is best-effort.** Where only abstracts are accessible, extraction is from the abstract; this is recorded as `medium` confidence.
- **No risk-of-bias scoring.** Studies are characterized by `extraction_confidence` (which captures source-quality coarsely), not a formal QUADAS / Newcastle-Ottawa instrument.
- **No effect modification analysis** beyond the pre-specified dose-stratified sensitivity.

These limitations are accepted because (a) the goal is to inform a decision between two well-defined paths (11.3a vs 11.3b), not to publish a standalone meta-analysis, and (b) a more formal protocol would inflate scope beyond v0.3 milestone reasonable bounds. The honest reporting of these limitations is what makes the resulting decision defensible.
